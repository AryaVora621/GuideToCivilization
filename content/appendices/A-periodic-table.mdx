This appendix provides a comprehensive reference to the periodic table, organized by groups and periods, with focus on abundance, practical uses, and identification. Post-collapse civilization depends on understanding which elements are common, extractable, and useful.

## Part 6: Toxicity and Safety Notes

Post-collapse civilization must recognize hazardous elements to avoid them.

| Element | Hazard | Primary Risk | Avoidance |
|---------|--------|-------------|-----------|
| Lead | Neurotoxic | Absorption through skin, ingestion | Avoid using in vessels, ammunition primers |
| Mercury | Neurotoxic (vapor) | Inhalation, skin contact | Use sealed barometers; avoid spills |
| Arsenic | Acute toxin | Ingestion, inhalation | Do not use in pesticides or food |
| Thallium | Acute toxin | Ingestion, skin absorption | Avoid completely |
| Cadmium | Bioaccumulative | Ingestion, inhalation | Avoid in food-contact items |
| Polonium | Radioactive, extreme toxin | Inhalation, ingestion | Avoid completely (research only) |
| Plutonium | Radioactive, extreme toxin | Inhalation, ingestion, skin contact | Do not handle (weapons/fuel only) |

## Part 7: Element Extraction Methods (Overview)

Quick reference for obtaining elements from natural sources.

| Element | Ore/Source | Simple Extraction | Difficulty |
|---------|-----------|-------------------|-----------|
| Iron | Hematite (Fe₂O₃), magnetite | Charcoal furnace reduction | Moderate |
| Copper | Chalcopyrite (CuFeS₂), native | Heat/charcoal with flux | Easy |
| Tin | Cassiterite (SnO₂) | Charcoal furnace reduction | Easy |
| Lead | Galena (PbS) | Roasting then reduction | Easy |
| Gold | Native (nuggets, sand) | Pan-washing, fire assay | Very easy |
| Silver | Native, argentite (Ag₂S) | Cyanide leaching or mercury amalgamation | Moderate |
| Zinc | Sphalerite (ZnS) | High-temperature distillation | Hard |
| Aluminum | Bauxite (Al₂O₃·xH₂O) | Electrolysis (impossible without electricity) | Extremely hard |
| Sulfur | Native deposits, pyrite | Mine/dig, roast pyrite | Easy |
| Nitrogen | Atmosphere (78%) | Haber-Bosch process (requires electricity) | Extremely hard |
