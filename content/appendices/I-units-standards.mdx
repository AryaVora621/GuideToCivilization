## Units, Standards & Measurement

A quick-reference for measurement across all volumes: the SI system, conversion factors to and from imperial and older units, fundamental physical constants, and practical standards for length, mass, time, temperature, and more. Measurement is the foundation of science, trade, and engineering; a society that standardizes its units can build, trade, and reproduce results reliably.

## The SI Base Units

All physical quantities derive from seven base units. Memorize these; everything else is built from them.

| Quantity | Unit | Symbol | Rough definition |
|----------|------|--------|------------------|
| Length | metre | m | Distance light travels in 1/299,792,458 second |
| Mass | kilogram | kg | Fixed by the Planck constant (formerly a metal cylinder) |
| Time | second | s | 9,192,631,770 oscillations of a cesium atom |
| Electric current | ampere | A | Defined via the elementary charge |
| Temperature | kelvin | K | Defined via the Boltzmann constant |
| Amount of substance | mole | mol | 6.02214076 times 10^23 entities |
| Luminous intensity | candela | cd | Luminous power in a direction |

## Temperature Conversions

Temperature uses offset scales, so use formulas, not simple multipliers.

| Convert | Formula |
|---------|---------|
| Celsius to Fahrenheit | F = (C times 9/5) + 32 |
| Fahrenheit to Celsius | C = (F minus 32) times 5/9 |
| Celsius to Kelvin | K = C + 273.15 |
| Kelvin to Celsius | C = K minus 273.15 |

Key reference points:

| Point | Celsius | Fahrenheit | Kelvin |
|-------|---------|------------|--------|
| Absolute zero | -273.15 | -459.67 | 0 |
| Water freezes | 0 | 32 | 273.15 |
| Room temperature | 20 | 68 | 293.15 |
| Human body | 37 | 98.6 | 310.15 |
| Water boils (sea level) | 100 | 212 | 373.15 |
| Lead melts | 327 | 621 | 600 |

## Establishing Standards Without Instruments

If you must bootstrap measurement from nothing, reproduce these natural references:

1. **Length:** Water ties length and mass together: one litre (a cube 10 cm on a side) of pure water at 4 °C has a mass of one kilogram. Build a cube, fill with water, and you have both a volume and a mass standard.
2. **Mass:** As above — 1 cubic centimetre of water is 1 gram; 1 litre is 1 kilogram.
3. **Time:** A pendulum about 0.994 metres long swings one way in one second at sea level. Tune a pendulum to a 2-second full period to define the second.
4. **Temperature:** Melting ice (0 °C) and boiling water at sea level (100 °C) give two fixed, reproducible points; divide between them.
5. **Right angle:** The 3-4-5 triangle (sides in that ratio) always forms a true right angle — the surveyor's and builder's check.

## Practical Conversion Tips

- Carry the key "anchor" factors (2.54 cm/inch, 0.4536 kg/lb, 3.785 L/gal, 1.609 km/mile) and derive the rest.
- Always record which system and which variant (US vs imperial) a measurement uses; mismatched units have destroyed spacecraft and bridges.
- For trade, agree on a single standard set of weights and measures and protect the reference standards from tampering.
- Temperature, unlike length or mass, needs offset formulas — never just multiply.
