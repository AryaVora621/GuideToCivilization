## Animal Identification and Field Tracking Reference

This appendix provides visual identification keys, track patterns, scat characteristics, and behavioral clues for mammals, birds, and reptiles. Use this reference for wildlife observation, hunting, trapping, and ecological monitoring.

## Mammal Identification by Size Class

### Large Mammals (above 100 lbs / 45 kg)

| Animal | Body Length | Height at Shoulder | Fur/Coloring | Distinctive Features |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Deer (white-tailed) | 5–7 feet | 3–3.5 feet | Brown-gray, white tail underside | Bounding gait; raised white tail when fleeing |
| Moose | 8–10 feet | 5–6.5 feet | Dark brown to blackish | Massive antlers (males); humped shoulders; long legs |
| Black bear | 5–6 feet | 2.5–3 feet standing | Black or brown | Rounded ears; straight snout; heavy build |
| Grizzly bear | 6–7 feet | 3.5–4 feet standing | Brown with silvery tips | Dished face; shoulder hump; longer claws visible |
| Wolf | 4.5–6.5 feet | 2.5–3 feet | Gray, tan, brown (variable) | Large paws; narrow chest; long legs; pack behavior |
| Mountain lion (puma) | 6–8 feet (with tail) | 2–2.5 feet | Tawny to reddish-brown | Long tail; small rounded head; solitary |
| Elk | 7.5–9.5 feet | 4–5 feet | Golden-brown to gray | Large antlers; rump patch lighter colored |

### Medium Mammals (25–100 lbs / 11–45 kg)

| Animal | Body Length | Fur/Coloring | Signs (Scat, Burrows) | Behavior |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Bobcat | 2–2.5 feet | Brown-gray spotted | Scat under cover; scrapes on ground | Solitary; nocturnal; hisses when threatened |
| Coyote | 3–3.5 feet | Gray-brown, sandy | Scat on prominent rocks/trails | Hunts alone or pairs; howling at dawn/dusk |
| Raccoon | 1.5–2 feet | Gray with black mask | Scat near water; 5-toed prints | Nocturnal; hands-like paws; enters garbage |
| Badger | 2–2.5 feet | Black with white stripes | Burrows with mounded entrances | Stocky; aggressive if cornered; digs constantly |
| Fox (red) | 2–2.5 feet | Red with black legs/ears, white tail tip | Scat on prominent spots | Solitary; hunts alone; runs with tail straight |
| Porcupine | 2–3 feet | Dark brown with visible quills | Scat clusters; trees stripped of bark | Slow mover; nocturnal; waddles when walking |
| Beaver | 3–4 feet (with tail) | Brown; flat paddle tail | Dammed streams; felled trees; lodges | Semi-aquatic; bucks called males; kits born in lodge |

### Small Mammals (under 25 lbs / 11 kg)

| Animal | Body Length | Identifying Feature | Scat / Track Size | Habitat |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Squirrel (tree) | 8–12 inches + bushy tail | Bushy tail; tufted ears (some species) | Scattered nut shells; no scat in latrine | Trees; caches nuts in ground |
| Ground squirrel | 5–9 inches | Stripes or spots; short tail | Dirt mounds at burrow | Open meadows; colonial burrows |
| Rabbit/hare | 12–20 inches | Large ears; hopped prints | Oval pellets; large hind prints | Grassland/forest edge; breeds spring |
| Weasel (long-bodied) | 7–12 inches + tail | Slender body; dark brown | Scat in scent posts; tiny prints | Woods; hunts rodents; kills for surplus |
| Mink | 12–18 inches | Semi-aquatic; dark brown | Scat near water; webbed feet | Near streams/ponds; eats fish, muskrat |
| Otter | 2–3 feet + tail | Aquatic; smooth brown fur; large webbed feet | Scat in distinctive latrines; eel remains | Rivers, coasts; playful; slides in snow/mud |
| Muskrat | 16–25 inches | Aquatic rodent; naked tail | Round lodges in marshes; plant clippings | Marshes/wetlands; swims with tail |
| Porcupine quill | — | Barbed quill; micro-grooves hold fast | 1.5–4 inches long | Defense mechanism; quills re-grow |

## Track Identification Chart (Footprints)

### Common Track Patterns (Snow/Mud)

| Animal | Front Foot | Hind Foot | Walking Pattern | Claw Marks |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Deer (hoofed) | Single cloven hoof (2 parts) | Single hoof | Straight line (direct register) | No claws visible |
| Bear (front) | 5 toes, large pad | Hind foot longer, 5 toes | Pigeon-toed front; hind overlaps front | Claws show, under 1 inch |
| Wolf | 4 toes | 4 toes | Straight line; direct register | Claws visible; large paw under 5 inches |
| Mountain lion | 4 toes | 4 toes (front larger) | Straight line; tail drag sometimes visible | Claws retracted (feline) |
| Fox | 4 toes | 4 toes | Nearly straight line | Small claw marks; prints under 2 inches |
| Raccoon | 5 toes, hand-like | 5 toes, hand-like | Side-by-side, meandering | Claws visible, tiny nails |
| Squirrel | 4 toes (front tiny) | 5 toes (hind large) | Bounding pattern; hind prints ahead | Small prints under 2 inches |
| Rabbit | 4 toes (front) | 4 toes (hind very large) | Bounding; hind feet land ahead | No claw marks visible |

## Scat (Feces) Identification

### Scat Characteristics by Species

| Animal | Size/Shape | Color | Texture | Additional Signs |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Deer | Pellets, 0.5–0.75 inches | Brown to black | Hard; individual droppings | Grouped in small areas |
| Bear | Varies greatly 1–2 inches diameter | Brown, black, or variable | Contains plant material or fur | Massive; often on trails |
| Wolf | 0.5–1 inch diameter, tapered | Gray to tan | Contains hair, bone fragments | Often on prominent rocks (scent marking) |
| Mountain lion | 0.75–1.5 inches | Brown to black | Contains hair and bone | Rarely scats; buried or scattered |
| Fox | 0.5–0.75 inches, tapered | Gray-brown | Hair and seeds | Deposited on prominent spots |
| Raccoon | 0.5–1 inch, tubular | Variable | Seeds and plant matter | Often in communal latrines; near water |
| Coyote | 0.75–1.25 inches | Gray-brown | Contains hair and plant matter | On trails and rocks; strong odor |
| Badger | 0.75–1 inch, tubular | Variable | Fur, feathers, insect remains | At burrow entrances |
| Otter | 0.5–1 inch, with fish scales | Greenish-gray | Fish bones, scales, shells | In distinctive latrines; fishy smell |
| Porcupine | Pellets 0.25 inches | Brown-black | Plant material | Under feeding sites; scattered |
| Pika | Tiny pellets, under 0.25 inches | Dark brown | Grass and plant matter | Near rocky outcrops; prolific |

## Reptile Identification (Snakes and Lizards)

### Venomous vs. Non-Venomous Snakes (North America)

| Snake Type | Venomous Species (N. America) | Head Shape | Pupil Shape | Heat-Sensing Pits |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Pit vipers | Rattlesnake, copperhead, cottonmouth | Triangular/arrow-shaped | Vertical (cat-like) | Visible pits between eye and nostril |
| Coral snake | Coral snake (rare) | Rounded | Round | None visible |
| Non-venomous | Garter, king, milk, rat, water snakes | Rounded | Round | None |

**Coral snake rule:** "Red on yellow, kill a fellow; red on black, friend of Jack" (only applies in southern US; rare in other regions)

## Behavioral Sign Recognition

### Rubs and Scratches

| Sign | Animal | Meaning | Season |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tree rub (bark stripped high) | Deer or elk | Males marking territory; antler velvet removal | Fall (rut season) |
| Antler rub (small trees) | Deer or elk | Scent marking and velvet removal | Late summer–fall |
| Claw marks on tree | Bear | Territorial marking; climbing practice | Spring–summer |
| Scratch posts (ground) | Cat (wild or domestic) | Scent marking; claw maintenance | Year-round |
| Log scratch | Badger | Claw marks at burrow entrance | Spring–summer |

### Feeding Signs

| Sign | Animals | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|
| Bark stripped from trees | Deer, rabbits, squirrels, porcupines | Winter food; identifies herbivory level |
| Scattered pine cones (stripped) | Squirrels, birds | Feeding activity; cone scales discarded |
| Nut shells (cracked open) | Squirrels, badgers | Predation on seeds; small vs. large cracks |
| Fish remains at water's edge | Otter, mink, marten | Recent kill; identifies predator by size |
| Feathers scattered (field kill) | Raptors, foxes, coyotes | Predation; identifies predator by method |
| Scat on prominent rocks | Carnivores (wolf, coyote, fox) | Territorial marking; scent communication |

## Nocturnal vs. Diurnal Activity Patterns

### Expected Activity Times (Temperate Regions)

| Animal | Primary Activity | Secondary Activity | Always Avoid |
|---|---|---|---|
| Deer | Dawn/dusk (crepuscular) | Active midday in cool weather | Rarely active at night in summer |
| Bear | Dusk to dawn (nocturnal) | Diurnal in cool seasons | Daytime rest sites (dangerous) |
| Wolf | Dusk to dawn | Active any time when hunting | Predictable patterns; variable |
| Mountain lion | Dusk to midnight | Occasionally diurnal in remote areas | Encounters rare; most active at night |
| Fox | Nocturnal | Dusk/dawn active in winter | Rarely seen mid-day |
| Raccoon | Nocturnal | Occasionally active dusk/dawn | Daytime den sites |
| Badger | Nocturnal | Active dusk/dawn | Underground dens |
| Coyote | Nocturnal to crepuscular | Active any time | Most vocal at dusk/dawn |
| Rabbit | Crepuscular | Most active at dawn | Resting in brush during day |
| Squirrel | Diurnal | Midday rest during heat | Nocturnal activity rare |

## Safety Guidelines for Wildlife Encounters

### Distance and Approach Rules (Recommended Minimum Distances)

| Animal | Minimum Distance | Reason | Reaction Zone |
|---|---|---|---|
| Deer | 50 yards | Flight response; can injure if cornered | Bounding retreat; alert posture at under 100 yards |
| Bear | 100 yards (at minimum) | Defensive/unpredictable; mothers dangerous | Aggression likely if under 50 yards; charge possible |
| Mountain lion | Not applicable | Avoid encounters entirely; rare | Attack risk below 20 yards (extreme danger) |
| Wolf | 100+ yards | Pack defense; predatory risk in some regions | Avoidance likely; attack risk minimal but possible |
| Moose | 50 yards | Can charge; dangerous antlers and kick | Defensive if calves present; avoid mothers |
| Rattlesnake | 6+ feet | Venom threat; warning rattle | Strike range under 3 feet; give wide berth |

### Bear Encounter Protocol

1. If distant (under 1/4 mile): Back away slowly; do not run
2. If close (under 100 yards): Use bear spray if available; aim for face
3. If attacked: Play dead for black bears; fight back against grizzly
4. Never: Chase, corner, surprise, or approach cubs
