## Overview

Books preserve knowledge in portable, durable form. A bound book lasts centuries if kept reasonably dry. Bookbinding is a practical craft that allows settlements to preserve written knowledge: recipes, technical information, histories, stories.

By month 2, a settlement can bind its first books and begin a library. By year 2, a robust production of bound books allows knowledge to be archived and transmitted to future generations.

## Materials & Tools

**For pages:**

- Parchment (animal skin) or paper (plant fibers)
- Ink (for writing; see Vol 4: Language & Communication)

**For binding:**

- Thread or sinew (for sewing signatures)
- Leather or cloth (for covers)
- Boards (wood or cardboard)
- Glue (hide glue, bone glue)

**For tools:**

- Bone folder (smoothing folds)
- Needle (sewing signatures)
- Cutting tools (knife, shears)
- Ruler (marking guidelines)

## Step-by-Step Instructions

### Step 1: Prepare Pages (Signatures)

Pages are sewn together in bundles called signatures.

**Signature construction:**

1. **Gather pages:**
   - Folded parchment or paper (4 to 8 leaves per signature)
   - Folded in half creates nested pages
   - Each sheet = 4 pages (front and back, both sides of fold)

2. **Example signature:**
   - Take 4 sheets of parchment
   - Fold each sheet in half
   - Nest them together (innermost sheet first, then next layer around it)
   - Result: one signature with 16 pages

3. **Mark sewing points:**
   - Use a bone folder to crease the fold
   - Mark 3 to 5 points along the fold (spine) where thread will pass
   - Spacing: 1 to 2 cm apart

4. **Prepare multiple signatures:**
   - Create 3 to 10 signatures per book (varies by book size)
   - Stack signatures in order (first signature, second, third, etc.)

### Step 2: Sew Signatures Together

Signatures are stitched to create the book block.

**Sewing procedure:**

1. **Stack signatures:**
   - Arrange in order
   - Hold together (one person holds while another sews, or use a sewing frame)

2. **Thread needle:**
   - Use thread or sinew approximately 1 meter long
   - Thread through needle

3. **Sewing pattern (saddle stitch, simplest):**
   - Start from the inside of the first signature
   - Push needle through the first sewing hole from inside
   - Pull thread out (leaving 10 cm tail inside)
   - Go around the spine and back through the same hole
   - Move to next hole and repeat

4. **Connect signatures:**
   - After sewing the first signature, continue to the second
   - Thread passes through both signatures at each hole
   - Creates strong binding

5. **Finish:**
   - After sewing all signatures, tie the thread ends together
   - Excess thread is trimmed

**Result:**

- All signatures stitched together
- Book block holds together firmly
- Spine shows a series of knots or stitches

### Step 3: Create the Cover

Covers protect the pages and make the book durable.

**Cover construction (leather or cloth):**

1. **Select material:**
   - Leather (most durable, lasts centuries)
   - Cloth (acceptable, lasts decades)
   - Wood boards (optional inner structure)

2. **Prepare boards:**
   - Cut two wooden or cardboard boards (same size as book height/width)
   - Boards are glued to the inside of the cover material

3. **Create cover material:**
   - Cut leather or cloth 2 cm larger than the boards (allows folding at edges)
   - Score (crease) the leather to help it fold

4. **Glue components together:**
   - Glue wooden boards to the inside of the leather/cloth
   - Allow glue to dry (hide glue dries slowly; 24 hours)
   - Fold edges of leather/cloth around boards and glue

5. **Decorate (optional):**
   - Tooling: use heated tools to press patterns into leather
   - Painting: paint title or decoration on the cover
   - Stamping: use stamps to press designs into leather

### Step 4: Attach Cover to Book Block

The book block (sewn pages) is glued to the inside of the cover.

**Attachment procedure:**

1. **Prepare endpapers:**
   - Blank pages attached to the inside of the front and back covers
   - Acts as a hinge between cover and pages
   - One blank page glued to the inside of the cover
   - One blank page glued to the first page of the book block

2. **Glue endpapers:**
   - Apply hide glue to the endpaper
   - Press firmly against the cover interior
   - Hold for several seconds
   - Allow glue to dry

3. **Spine reinforcement (optional):**
   - Glue cloth or leather strip along the spine
   - Provides additional strength
   - Improves durability

### Step 5: Create Special Bindings (Advanced)

Alternative binding methods for special purposes.

**Clamshell case binding (for valuable books):**

1. **Build a protective case:**
   - Wooden box lined with cloth
   - Book sits inside, protected
   - Case has a lid that closes over the book

2. **Advantages:**
   - Maximum protection from damage
   - Allows books to be stored without shelves
   - Beautiful presentation

**Map or ledger binding (flat-lying books):**

1. **Attach binding to the side instead of the spine:**
   - Allows books to lie flat
   - Important for ledgers and maps
   - Requires different sewing and hinge design

**Japanese stitch binding (simple, no glue):**

1. **Sew pages with exposed stitching:**
   - Thread visible along the spine
   - Decorative and simple
   - No glue needed (stronger than expected)

### Step 6: Organize a Bindery

Establish a workshop for book production.

**Bindery workshop setup:**

1. **Work surfaces:**
   - Cutting table (stable, flat)
   - Sewing frame or stand
   - Pressing board (for gluing and drying)

2. **Tool storage:**
   - Organized by type
   - Sharp tools protected
   - Glues stored properly (hide glue in sealed containers)

3. **Materials inventory:**
   - Pages (parchment or paper) in storage
   - Leather and cloth for covers
   - Thread and sinew
   - Glues

4. **Organization:**
   - Production pipeline: prepare pages → sew → cover → finish
   - Several books in progress at once
   - Assign roles: cutters, sewers, binders, finishers

### Step 7: Preserve Finished Books

Books require care to last centuries.

**Storage conditions:**

1. **Temperature and humidity:**
   - Stable environment preferred
   - Avoid extreme heat or cold (causes warping)
   - Avoid high humidity (causes mold) or very dry air (causes cracking)
   - Ideal: 15 to 20°C, 40 to 60% relative humidity

2. **Light protection:**
   - Keep books away from direct sunlight
   - UV light fades leather and paper
   - Store in dim conditions if possible

3. **Organization:**
   - Shelves or cabinets (keep books upright or flat)
   - Organize by subject for easy retrieval
   - Keep inventory/catalog of books

4. **Regular inspection:**
   - Check for mold, insects, or damage
   - Repair small damage before it worsens
   - Rebind books if covers fail

**Lifespan:**

- Well-made leather-bound books: 500+ years
- Well-made cloth-bound books: 100 to 200 years
- Parchment pages: 1000+ years if properly stored
- Paper pages: 100 to 300 years (depends on paper quality)

## Verification

**Success looks like:**

- Sewn signatures hold together firmly
- Cover is attached securely and lies flat
- Pages open easily without tearing
- Book is square and even (not warped)
- Leather or cloth cover is smooth and durable
- Book lies open without forcing

**Failure looks like:**

- Pages fall out (poor sewing or gluing)
- Cover separates from pages
- Spine cracks or breaks
- Pages wrinkle or warp
- Stitching is loose or visible from outside (poor technique)

## Common Mistakes

1. **"I'll make signatures too thick"** — Thick signatures are hard to sew evenly. Keep to 8 or fewer leaves per signature.

2. **"I won't crease the folds; pages will hold together"** — Crisp creases are essential for even sewing and durability. Use a bone folder.

3. **"I'll use weak thread or sinew"** — Strong thread is essential. Test before using; should not break under tension.

4. **"I won't reinforce the spine"** — Spine bears most stress. Reinforce with cloth or leather strip.

5. **"I'll glue the cover while the pages are still damp"** — Pages must be completely dry. Moisture prevents glue from setting.

6. **"I'll make the cover boards too flexible"** — Boards must be stiff to protect pages. Use thick board or laminate thinner boards.

7. **"I won't test the glue on samples first"** — Test glues and methods on scrap materials. Mistakes on finished books are permanent.

8. **"I'll store books carelessly; they'll survive"** — Books require proper storage (stable temperature, dry, dark). Neglect destroys them.
