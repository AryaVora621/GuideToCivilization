## Overview

Civilization does not appear overnight. It develops in stages, each building on the previous. Understanding the stages helps you plan development and avoid skipping necessary steps.

These 16 stages describe the progression from immediate post-collapse survival to functioning advanced civilization.

## The 16 Stages

**Stage 1: Immediate Survival (0-1 month)**

Focus: Water, food, shelter
Goal: Do not die
Skills needed: Water purification, basic food procurement, shelter construction
Volume references: Vol 2-5 (Survival essentials)

**Stage 2: Stability (1-3 months)**

Focus: Reliable food and water, defended location
Goal: Consistent daily needs
Skills needed: Well-digging, storage, basic defense
Volume references: Vol 1-5, Ch 1-2; Vol 9, Ch 1

**Stage 3: Organized Group (3-6 months)**

Focus: Group coordination, basic rules, leadership
Goal: 5-20 people working together reliably
Skills needed: Leadership, basic governance, group psychology
Volume references: Vol 7, Ch 1-2; Vol 8, Ch 1

**Stage 4: Food Production (6-12 months)**

Focus: Agriculture or herding begins
Goal: 50%+ of food from own production
Skills needed: Crop growing, animal husbandry, soil management
Volume references: Vol 9 (all), Vol 11 (veterinary)

**Stage 5: Surplus (12-18 months)**

Focus: Producing more than immediate needs
Goal: Food surplus for trade or storage
Skills needed: Crop rotation, preservation, storage
Volume references: Vol 9, Ch 3-5; Vol 12 (food science)

**Stage 6: Craft Specialization (18-24 months)**

Focus: Some people do crafts instead of pure farming
Goal: Tools, textiles, pottery made locally
Skills needed: Crafts (ceramics, textiles, tool-making)
Volume references: Vol 15-17 (crafts), Vol 33 (metallurgy)

**Stage 7: Trade Networks (2-3 years)**

Focus: Exchange with other settlements
Goal: Regular trade for items not produced locally
Skills needed: Negotiation, measurement standards, trust-building
Volume references: Vol 25 (trade, money)

**Stage 8: Formal Governance (2-3 years)**

Focus: Written laws, institutions, not personality-based
Goal: Government functions without the original leader
Skills needed: Legal systems, administration, succession planning
Volume references: Vol 8 (all), Vol 6, Ch 4-6

**Stage 9: Infrastructure (3-5 years)**

Focus: Roads, water systems, defensive works
Goal: Permanent physical improvements
Skills needed: Engineering, surveying, project management
Volume references: Vol 18-20 (engineering)

**Stage 10: Knowledge Preservation (3-5 years)**

Focus: Writing and storing knowledge systematically
Goal: Knowledge survives individuals
Skills needed: Writing, mathematics, scientific method
Volume references: Vol 22-24 (knowledge), Vol 21 (scientific method)

**Stage 11: Education Systems (5-10 years)**

Focus: Formal teaching of youth
Goal: Next generation trained without direct apprenticeship
Skills needed: Teaching methods, curriculum design
Volume references: Vol 7, Ch 5-6 (education)

**Stage 12: Art and Culture (5-10 years)**

Focus: Non-essential activities (music, visual arts, storytelling)
Goal: Cultural identity and morale
Skills needed: Arts, music, cultural preservation
Volume references: Vol 20 (arts and culture)

**Stage 13: Advanced Crafts (7-15 years)**

Focus: Complex manufactured goods (advanced metallurgy, fine textiles)
Goal: Produce complex tools and goods locally
Skills needed: Advanced metallurgy, precision crafts
Volume references: Vol 33-34 (advanced materials)

**Stage 14: Medicine and Health (10+ years)**

Focus: Systematic health practices beyond emergency treatment
Goal: Population health improves, child mortality decreases
Skills needed: Diagnosis, anatomy, pharmacology
Volume references: Vol 14 (all), Vol 38 (pharmacology)

**Stage 15: Scientific Inquiry (15+ years)**

Focus: Experimentation and discovery, not just recorded knowledge
Goal: New knowledge created, not just preserved
Skills needed: Scientific method, mathematics, systematic observation
Volume references: Vol 21 (scientific method), Vol 22-27 (sciences)

**Stage 16: Advanced Civilization (20+ years)**

Focus: Complex systems, trade, law, science, art all functioning
Goal: Functioning civilization approaching pre-collapse capability
Skills needed: All of the above plus complex systems management
Volume references: Vol 1-45 mastery

## Progress Indicators

**How to know you are at each stage:**

- Stage 1: You have water, food, shelter today
- Stage 2: You have reliable water, food, shelter every day
- Stage 3: You have rules and leadership accepted by most
- Stage 4: You harvest significant food crops
- Stage 5: You store food for winter and trade
- Stage 6: Non-farmers work in crafts
- Stage 7: You trade regularly with other settlements
- Stage 8: Government functions without founder
- Stage 9: You have permanent roads, irrigation, or walls
- Stage 10: You write and teach from written documents
- Stage 11: Most children attend formal education
- Stage 12: You have musicians, artists, ceremonial events
- Stage 13: You manufacture complex goods locally
- Stage 14: Infant mortality is low, life expectancy increasing
- Stage 15: You conduct experiments and discover new things
- Stage 16: You have writing, law, trade, science, art, government all functioning

## Time Estimates

**Survival (Stages 1-5):** 12-18 months with good conditions

**Building (Stages 6-10):** 3-7 years with 50+ people and stable resources

**Advancement (Stages 11-16):** 10-30 years with sustained population and knowledge

**Critical factors affecting speed:**

- Population size (larger groups build faster)
- Available knowledge (from guides like this)
- Stability (conflicts slow progress)
- Resources (good land and water accelerate)
- Weather (harsh climates slow stages 1-5)

## Common Mistakes

**Mistake 1: Skipping stages**

You want universities (Stage 15) before you have food surplus (Stage 5). Stages build on each other; skipping causes collapse.

**Mistake 2: Pushing too fast**

You rush Stage 4 (agriculture). Farmers burn out; soil depletes. Stages need time to solidify.

**Mistake 3: Mistaking activity for progress**

You have 50 people doing things, but only 20 actually pulling weight. Groups still at Stage 3 psychologically, even if Stage 5 physically.

**Mistake 4: Forgetting about succession**

Stage 3 leader dies. Group collapses to Stage 1. Succession planning is essential before reaching Stage 8.

**Mistake 5: Assuming linear progress**

A disaster (plague, harvest failure, conflict) can drop you backward one or two stages. Plan for setbacks.
