## Overview

This guide contains 45 volumes covering everything needed to rebuild civilization after collapse. It is organized by topic, not chronologically. This chapter explains how to navigate it.

Whether you need immediate survival knowledge or long-term civilization building, this guide has a path for you.

## How the Guide Is Organized

**45 volumes organized by topic:**

- **Volumes 1-5:** Survival and immediate needs (water, shelter, food, medicine, sanitation)
- **Volumes 6-13:** Foundations of knowledge (ethics, governance, agriculture, veterinary medicine, food science)
- **Volumes 14-20:** Engineering and skilled crafts (mining, ceramics, textiles, materials, architecture, hydraulics, arts)
- **Volumes 21-27:** Core sciences (scientific method, mathematics, language, astronomy, biology)
- **Volumes 28-45:** Advanced topics (hydraulics, energy, electronics, materials science, telecommunications)

**Each volume has 6-8 chapters** covering progressively deeper aspects of the topic.

**Each chapter follows a standard structure:**
- Overview: Why this matters
- Prerequisites: What you should know first
- Materials & Tools: What you need
- Step-by-Step Instructions: How to do it
- Verification: How to know if it worked
- Common Mistakes: Pitfalls to avoid
- Cross-References: Related chapters

## How to Find What You Need

**If you know the topic:**

1. Find the volume number for that topic (see volume list)
2. Read chapters 1-3 for basic knowledge
3. Read later chapters for deeper understanding

**If you need immediate help:**

See "Emergency Paths" below.

**If you want comprehensive knowledge:**

Start with Volume 1 (foundation) and work forward.

**To find a specific skill:**

Search the index by keyword (water, crops, weapons, medicine, etc.).

## Learning Paths

### Emergency Path (Immediate Survival)

If collapse is happening now and you need to survive:

1. **Vol 1: Water** - Ch 1-2 (finding and purifying water)
2. **Vol 2: Shelter** - Ch 1-2 (building basic shelter)
3. **Vol 3: Food** - Ch 1-2 (foraging and hunting)
4. **Vol 4: Medicine** - Ch 1-2 (treating injuries and illness)
5. **Vol 5: Sanitation** - Ch 1 (preventing disease)

Time to read: 4-6 hours
Survival probability improvement: 300%

### Settlement Path (Building a Community)

If you are establishing a settlement and want to build it sustainably:

1. **Vol 1-5:** Survival fundamentals (all chapters)
2. **Vol 6: Ethics & Philosophy** - Ch 1-4 (how to organize)
3. **Vol 7: Psychology** - Ch 1-4 (understanding people)
4. **Vol 8: Governance** - Ch 1-4 (systems and laws)
5. **Vol 9: Agriculture** - Ch 1-5 (producing food)
6. **Vol 14: Medicine** - All chapters (healing)
7. **Vol 18: Architecture** - Ch 1-3 (building structures)

Time to read: 30-40 hours
Enables: Functioning settlement of 100+ people

### Knowledge Preservation Path

If you want to record and preserve knowledge:

1. **Vol 6: Ethics** - Ch 2 (what knowledge matters)
2. **Vol 21: Scientific Method** - All chapters
3. **Vol 22: Mathematics** - Ch 1-2
4. **Vol 23: Language & Writing** - All chapters
5. **Vol 24: Surveying** - Ch 1
6. **Vol 29: Books & Preservation** (when created)

Time to read: 15-20 hours
Result: Organized knowledge system

### Advanced Civilization Path

For long-term civilization building (10+ years):

Read all 45 volumes in order. Start with Volumes 1-13 (foundation). Then read 14-45 for specialization.

## Recommended Reading Order

**If starting from scratch:**

1. Read Vol 1 entirely (understanding the structure)
2. Read Vols 2-5 (survival)
3. Read Vols 6-8 (organizing people)
4. Read Vols 9-13 (producing food and goods)
5. Specialize: Choose volumes matching your settlement's needs

**If already surviving:**

Start with Vol 6-8 (governance, psychology) to organize your group.

**If specializing in a field:**

Find the relevant volumes and read sequentially.

Example: To become a doctor, read:
- Vol 4: Medicine (all chapters)
- Vol 7: Ch 4 (psychology of healing)
- Vol 14: Ch 5-6 (anatomy, surgery)
- Vol 27: Ch 5 (human anatomy)

## Cross-References

Cross-references at the end of each chapter point to related chapters. Use them to deepen understanding.

Example: Learning agriculture (Vol 9)? Cross-reference to Vol 14 (medicine) covers disease in crops. Vol 18 (architecture) covers grain storage.

## Updating This Guide

This guide is not complete. New chapters will be added as knowledge is recovered and synthesized. Check back periodically.

If you discover errors or missing topics, record them. Future editors can improve the guide.

## Final Advice

**Start with your immediate need.** Do not try to read everything. Learn what you need to survive and thrive. As you stabilize, expand your knowledge.

**Share the guide.** Copy chapters and distribute. Knowledge should not be rare.

**Teach others.** The greatest preservation of knowledge is teaching it to the next generation.

The guide is a tool. Your understanding and application of it is what matters.
