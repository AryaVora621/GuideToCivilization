## Overview

Water is your most immediate threat. A person dies in three days without it. More die from drinking contaminated water than from most other post-collapse causes combined. Cholera, typhoid, dysentery, and giardia all travel in water that looks clean.

This chapter gives you five methods in order of what tools they require. Start with what you have. Improve when you can.

## Prerequisites

None. This chapter has no prerequisites. Read it first.

## Materials & Tools

**Method 1 (Boiling) — nothing but fire:**
- Container (metal pot, clay vessel, even a rock with a depression)
- Fire and fuel
- See Vol 1, Ch 2 (Fire Making) if needed

**Method 2 (Improvised filter) — materials you can find:**
- Three containers or a bifurcated container
- Layers from bottom to top: gravel, sand, crushed charcoal, fine sand, cloth or moss
- Container to catch filtered water

**Method 3 (Chlorine tablets or bleach):**
- Unscented household bleach (5–8% sodium hypochlorite) OR water purification tablets
- Dropper or small measuring spoon
- Container with lid

**Method 4 (Solar disinfection — SODIS):**
- Clear plastic or glass bottle (PET plastic, 2L max)
- Direct sunlight (minimum 6 hours; 2 days if overcast)

**Method 5 (Distillation) — best purity, most equipment:**
- Heat source
- Metal or clay vessel with lid or cover
- Tube or pipe to carry steam
- Collection vessel

## Step-by-Step Instructions

### Method 1: Boiling (Best for cloudy water + known contamination)

**Step 1: Pre-filter if water is visibly dirty.**
Pour through cloth, fine grass, or moss to remove large particles. Clarity is not the same as safety, but particles protect bacteria from heat.

**Step 2: Bring water to a rolling boil.**
A rolling boil (vigorous bubbling that cannot be stirred down) at sea level kills all pathogens including Giardia and Cryptosporidium. The water does not need to boil for 10 minutes — the rolling boil itself is the kill temperature. At elevations above 2,000 meters (6,500 feet), water boils at a lower temperature; boil for 3 minutes.

**Step 3: Let cool covered.**
Cover the container while cooling to prevent recontamination by insects or debris. Store in a covered, clean vessel.

### Method 2: Improvised Filtration (Use before boiling, not instead of it)

**Step 1: Build the filter stack.**
In a container with a hole in the bottom, layer from bottom to top:
- Cloth or moss (to hold layers in place)
- Fine sand (5 cm / 2 inches)
- Crushed charcoal from your fire (5 cm — NOT ash, charcoal)
- Coarse sand (5 cm)
- Gravel (5 cm)
- Cloth layer on top to catch debris

**Step 2: Pour water slowly through the top.**
Let it drip through. The first few liters will be discolored — discard them while the filter settles.

**Step 3: Collect output and then boil it.**
Filtration removes particles and some pathogens but does not sterilize. Always boil filtered water before drinking.

### Method 3: Chemical Treatment with Bleach

**Step 1: Start with clear water.**
Filter visibly dirty water first (Method 2). Bleach does not work well in turbid water.

**Step 2: Add bleach.**
Use unscented household bleach containing 5–8% sodium hypochlorite:
- Clear water: 8 drops per 1 liter (about 1/8 teaspoon)
- Cloudy water after filtering: 16 drops per 1 liter
- For bleach at 1% concentration, multiply the dose by 5–8x

**Step 3: Mix and wait 30 minutes.**
Stir, then leave covered for 30 minutes before drinking. The water should have a faint chlorine smell. If it has no smell, repeat the dose and wait another 15 minutes.

**Step 4: Aerate if taste is unpleasant.**
Pour between two containers several times to improve taste.

### Method 4: Solar Disinfection (SODIS)

**Step 1: Use the right bottle.**
PET plastic bottles (look for the recycling symbol with "1 PETE" on the bottom) up to 2 liters. Glass works too. Cloudy or scratched bottles work less well.

**Step 2: Pre-filter if water is turbid.**
SODIS only works in clear water. Use Method 2 first if needed.

**Step 3: Fill completely, remove air bubbles, seal.**
Air pockets reduce effectiveness.

**Step 4: Place on a reflective surface in direct sun.**
A corrugated metal roof works well. Minimum 6 hours in full sun. 2 days if mostly cloudy. This method does not work on completely overcast days.

**Step 5: Drink immediately or store in the bottle.**
Do not pour into other containers — the bottle itself is sterile inside.

### Method 5: Distillation (Removes salts, heavy metals, and all pathogens)

**Step 1: Build a still.**
Place water in a sealed metal pot. Attach a pipe or tube to an opening in the lid. Route the tube downward through cold water or air to condense steam. Collect drips at the end.

**Step 2: Heat to boiling.**
Steam rises, travels through the cooling tube, and condenses as pure water. Salt, minerals, and most contaminants remain in the original vessel.

**Step 3: Collect the distillate.**
Discard the first 50ml (it may contain volatile compounds). Collected water is safe to drink.

**Note:** Distillation removes beneficial minerals. For long-term sole water supply, add a small amount of wood ash (for potassium and minerals) to drinking water or ensure dietary mineral sources.

## Verification

**Boiling worked if:** Water reached a rolling boil and was kept covered.

**Filter worked if:** Output is visibly clear and you subsequently boiled it.

**Bleach worked if:** Water has a faint chlorine smell 30 minutes after treatment.

**SODIS worked if:** The bottle was clear PET plastic, the water was clear, and it had 6+ hours of direct sun.

**Signs of contamination still present:** Diarrhea, abdominal cramps, or nausea within 6–24 hours of drinking. Return to a different method.

## Common Mistakes

- **Thinking clear water is safe.** It is not. Most biological contamination is invisible.
- **Boiling for "10 minutes."** Unnecessary. A rolling boil is the kill point. Boiling longer wastes fuel.
- **Using scented bleach.** Additives can be toxic. Unscented only.
- **Using SODIS on cloudy days.** Does not work. Use bleach or boiling instead.
- **Storing purified water in a dirty container.** Recontamination. Use a clean, covered vessel.
- **Skipping pre-filtering before bleach.** Turbid water consumes the bleach before it disinfects.
